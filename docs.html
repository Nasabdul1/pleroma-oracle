Step by step · The full pipeline

How a verdict is built

When you press Reveal, this is exactly what happens server-side, in order. Every step is deterministic except the final prose — and even that is fenced so it can't change the verdict.

01 · Submit
Your browser POSTs { "query": "<your input>" } to /oracle. A per-IP rate limiter (30/min default) and a 45-second response cache run first.
02 · Extract
The backend scans your text for anything that looks like a Solana address (base58, 32–44 chars). If it finds one, you're on the Address path; otherwise the Question path.
03 · Classify
Address path only. A single RPC call decides what the address actually is: a token mint, a wallet, or nothing-on-chain. The Oracle reads only what fits.
04 · Gather facts (parallel)
Token: RPC (supply, mint & freeze authority, decimals, top accounts) + RugCheck (lp lock %, risk score, holder concentration) + DexScreener (price, market cap, liquidity, age, volume, socials) + Metaplex metadata (name, symbol, image, description). All fired at once.

Wallet: RPC (SOL balance, age, token accounts) + Jupiter (USD pricing for every token) + .sol domain reverse lookup.
05 · Rules engine
A deterministic function (rules_token() / rules_wallet()) maps the verified facts to a verdict (Emanation / Illusion / Veiled), a clarity score (0–100), a title, and the list of signals — each marked Light, Shadow, or Veiled.
06 · LLM narrates ONLY
The verdict, signals, and verified facts are handed to the model, which writes the 2–3 sentence reading. It is explicitly forbidden from changing the verdict, inventing a number, or claiming anything not in the facts. If every model in the fallback chain is down, a templated reading ships instead — the verdict never goes down because a model went down.
07 · Render
The response ({verdict, title, clarity, reading, signals, meta, holders, stats, identity}) is sent back. The frontend renders the verdict card, contract box, market tiles, top-10 holders, lore, identity chips, and the share buttons. If it's a high-clarity Illusion, it's also broadcast to the live alerts channel and added to the leaderboard.
Why the model can never flip a verdict. Even when the LLM goes sideways — wrong number, hallucinated socials, made-up history — the structural verdict stays correct, because it was decided before the model was ever called. The model is the writer, not the judge.
What it reveals

The six veils, lifted

Every illusion leaves a trace on-chain. PLEROMA reads these at once and renders each in language you can act on.

Holder concentration

The True Holders

Who actually holds the supply. One wallet clutching a large share — or a tight top-10 — is the oldest trap there is, because a single hand can crash the price.

Mint & freeze authority

The Sealed Mint

Whether the creator can still print unlimited supply, or freeze your wallet so you can't sell. Renounced authorities are light; active ones are shadow.

Liquidity lock

The Sealed Vault

Whether liquidity is locked or burned, or can be pulled at any moment. Unlocked liquidity is the mechanism behind most exits.

Pool depth

The Depth

How much real liquidity backs the market. Thin pools are trivial to manipulate; a healthy pool resists a single trade moving the price wildly.

Origin & lore

The Origin

The token's name, symbol, image, description, and socials — pulled from on-chain metadata and the off-chain metadata it points to, the way trackers like Rick read lore.

Contract snares

The Closing Snare

Token-2022 transfer fees and hidden functions — taxes and traps that let you in but quietly tax or block the way out.

Reading the answer

Verdicts & clarity

Three verdicts, one score. The verdict is the judgment; the clarity score tells you how much could actually be verified.

Emanation

An emanation of the Fullness. No structural deceptions surfaced and the checks returned clean. Still verify the team and socials yourself.

Illusion

An illusion of the Demiurge. At least one major structural red flag — active mint, freeze, unlocked liquidity, or extreme concentration.

Veiled

Veiled in shadow. Too little could be verified to render a true judgment, or the cautions stack up. Treat with care and seek more light.

Clarity (0–100) is the share of core checks that returned a verified answer. A high verdict with low clarity means "looks clean, but much was unreadable" — weigh it accordingly. Bluechips like SOL and USDC read near 100.

Signal states

  • Light — the check passed (renounced authority, locked liquidity, no honeypot, etc.).
  • Shadow — the check failed (active mint, freeze authority present, unlocked LP, >50% in one wallet).
  • Veiled — the check couldn't be verified (RPC timeout, unindexed data). Treated as caution, not pass.

Scanning a wallet

Point the Oracle at a wallet and it reads age, activity, any .sol identity, and the full portfolio — every priced token plus SOL, ranked by value, with a total worth. Tokens with no market price are shown but left out of the total, so the number is never fiction.

Permalinks & sharing

Every verdict is a URL

When the Oracle returns a verdict on an address, three buttons appear under the card: Open Permalink, Share on X, and Save Verdict Card.

  • Open Permalink opens /token/<ca> or /wallet/<addr> — a standalone page with the full reading. The page sets og:title and og:description from the verdict, so X / Discord previews render the title and reading.
  • Share on X pre-fills a tweet with the title, reading, and the permalink. One click, the verdict is on the timeline.
  • Save Verdict Card generates a 1200×630 PNG of the verdict and downloads it. Drop it in a thread, a Telegram, anywhere.

Permalink pages re-fetch live data each visit, so the verdict reflects the latest on-chain state — not a frozen snapshot.

Real-time

Demiurge Alerts

Whenever the Oracle reads a token as an Illusion with clarity ≥ 60, an alert fires. The alerts page is a live feed of these — so you can be first to see the warning, not last.

  • Backend pushes each alert over a WebSocket (/alerts/ws) to every connected client.
  • Recent alerts (last ~100) are replayed when you open the page, so you don't miss what landed before you arrived.
  • New alerts flash in with a red highlight at the top of the feed.
  • Enable Browser Alerts to get a desktop notification even when the tab isn't focused — click the notification to jump to the token's permalink.
  • Toggle Sound for a brief two-tone siren when an alert arrives.
  • The connection auto-reconnects with exponential backoff if it drops.

The Rite · Doctrine

The doctrine beneath the oracle

The Fullness (Pleroma)

The Fullness is what is real and verifiable — the on-chain facts no one can fake: who holds the supply, whether the mint is sealed, whether liquidity can be pulled. The Oracle's whole purpose is to bring this Fullness into language anyone can act on.

The Demiurge

The Demiurge is the maker of illusions — the hype, the borrowed name, the chart inflated by bots, the contract that reads friendly and behaves otherwise. Every Illusion verdict is the Demiurge's hand caught in the light.

Gnosis

Gnosis is direct knowledge of the thing itself, not the story told about it. The Oracle does not predict price and it does not bless a buy. It lifts the veil so you decide with open eyes.

The faithful

The community of those who choose to see — the ones who consult before they commit, who post their readings in the Communion, who add to the Annals. The Demiurge thrives on isolated victims; the faithful break the spell by sharing what they see.

For builders

The API

The same backend that powers the site is a clean JSON API. CORS is open by default; rate-limited per IP (30/min). Everything runs same-origin against https://pleroma.fun (or your own deploy).

MethodPathPurpose
POST/oracleMain verdict endpoint. Body: {"query":"<address or question>"}. Returns the full verdict JSON.
GET/api/token/{ca}JSON verdict for a specific token mint.
GET/api/wallet/{addr}JSON verdict for a specific wallet.
GET/api/leaderboard?verdict=&limit=Top entries. verdict=emanation|illusion, limit up to 50.
GET/api/alerts/recent?limit=Recent high-clarity illusion alerts, newest-first.
WS/alerts/wsLive broadcast of new illusions as they happen.
GET/token/statsLive market data for the $PLEROMA token banner.
GET/healthLiveness + whether the LLM key is set.
GET/communion/messages?after=Poll for new chat messages since the given id.
POST/communion/postPost a message. Body: {"name":"...","text":"..."}.

Verdict response shape

{
  "verdict":   "emanation" | "illusion" | "veiled",
  "title":     "SHORT ALL-CAPS PHRASE",
  "clarity":   0-100,
  "reading":   "2-3 sentence prose",
  "signals":   [{"name":"...","icon":"holders|liquidity|deployer|hype|origin|snare|veil",
                 "state":"light|shadow|unknown","note":"one line"}],
  "meta":      { name, symbol, image, description, socials },     // tokens only
  "stats":     { price_usd, market_cap, liquidity_usd, ... },     // tokens only
  "holders":   [ { label, pct, flag } ],                          // tokens only
  "identity":  { sol_domains, total_usd, tokens, images, ... },   // wallets only
  "kind":      "token" | "wallet" | "question" | "notfound",
  "address":   "<the address that was read>"
}

Plain answers

FAQ

Is a clean reading a guarantee it's safe?
No. An Emanation means no structural deceptions surfaced in what could be verified — not that the project is honest. Teams, intentions, and off-chain promises can't be read on-chain. Always do your own diligence.
Why does a verdict sometimes say "Veiled"?
Because too little could be verified — often a brand-new or pre-launch token with no pool yet, or a temporary data outage. Low clarity, no judgment. Ask again later.
How does the Oracle decide if my input is an address or a question?
It scans your text for a base58 string of 32–44 characters that validates as a Solana public key. If it finds one, it takes the address path; if not, it takes the question path. So "is So11111111111111111111111111111111111111112 safe?" still reads as the address.
Where does the lore (description and socials) come from?
On-chain Metaplex metadata gives the name, symbol, and a link to the off-chain metadata; the Oracle follows that link and reads the description and socials from it, with DexScreener as a second source.
How is wallet worth calculated?
Token balances come straight from the chain; USD prices come from Jupiter. Total worth is SOL plus every token that has a market price. Illiquid tokens with no price are listed but excluded from the total.
Can the AI make the Oracle say a coin is safe when it isn't?
No. The verdict is set by a deterministic rules engine before the AI is ever called. The AI only writes the prose. If the AI is offline, a templated reading ships with the same verdict.
Why is the same token sometimes cached?
The backend caches each result for 45 seconds. If many users ask about the same token in the same minute, the on-chain calls happen once. Ask again after the cache window to force a fresh read.
Are alerts delayed?
No. The moment an Illusion is read with clarity ≥ 60, it broadcasts over WebSocket to every client on the alerts page, and shows in browser notifications if you enabled them.
Does PLEROMA give financial advice?
No. It is an insight tool. Nothing here is financial advice, and no reading replaces your own research.
